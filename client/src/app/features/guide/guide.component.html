What is Touch Typing?

Touch typing is a typing technique where you type without looking at the keyboard. Your fingers rest on specific keys (home row) and move to reach other keys based on muscle memory.

Key Benefits:

  • Speed: Significantly faster typing speeds (60-100+ WPM)
  • Accuracy: Reduced errors and improved precision
  • Efficiency: Focus on content, not keyboard location
  • Ergonomics: Better hand positioning reduces strain
  • Productivity: Faster completion of typing tasks

How to Begin Touch Typing

1

Learn Home Row

Start with A, S, D, F (left hand) and J, K, L, ; (right hand)

2

Practice Basic Words

Begin with simple words using home row keys

3

Expand Gradually

Add keys outside home row one finger at a time

4

Build Speed

Focus on accuracy first, then increase speed

5

Practice Regularly

Consistent practice is key to developing muscle memory

6

Use Verbatim

Leverage our adaptive drills and AI insights for personalized practice

Recommendations:

  • Don't look at the keyboard - cover it if needed
  • Start slow and focus on accuracy over speed
  • Practice for short, regular sessions (15-30 minutes daily)
  • Use proper posture and hand positioning
  • Be patient - it takes time to develop muscle memory

Keyboard Zones

Top Row

Numbers and symbols (1-0, -, =, etc.) - Use your pinky and ring fingers to reach these keys. Keep your hands in the home row position and stretch up.

Home Row

ASDF (left hand) and JKL; (right hand) - This is your base position. Your fingers should always return here after pressing other keys.

Bottom Row

ZXCV (left hand) and NM,./ (right hand) - Use your pinky and ring fingers to reach down to these keys while maintaining home row position.
